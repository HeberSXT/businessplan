Executive Summary

Business Name: Soma XT

Business Description: Soma XT, in partnership with CQG, is developing a cutting-edge OTC trading platform that integrates trading, risk management, and margin call financing. This platform will cater to commodity hedgers, market makers, and financiers, providing a seamless, transparent, and efficient trading experience. Phase 1 leverages CQG's existing OTC platform, while Phase 2 focuses on complex, exotic, and structured products, along with advanced risk management and lifecycle management tools. Additionally, we plan to acquire a small software house with an existing OTC platform, codenamed "Project BluePrint," to accelerate our Phase 2 implementation and provide immediate revenue opportunities.

Mission Statement: To transform OTC trading by offering an open, agnostic platform that combines trading, risk management, and financing, enhancing transparency, efficiency, and accessibility for all market participants.

Vision: To be a leading force in the industry delivering integrated solutions that foster sustainable success and resilience amid the complexities of the commodities market.

Initial Target Market: Agricultural commodities sector in Brazil.

Financial Fortitude: With a solid R$60 million equity structure and a cost-efficient infrastructure, Soma XT stands as a self-sufficient entity. The allocation of an additional R$125 million in liquidity allows the company to extend around US$150 million in trading limits for customers. This initial balance sheet, coupled with proactive risk management strategies, underscores our commitment to mitigating market, credit, and liquidity risks.

Revenue Diversity and Expense Prudence: Our revenue model lies in a fully digital platform and commodities risk management funding. We ensure a diversified revenue stream from customers connected digitally to all tools needed to execute and finance trades. Our expense projections balance significant investment in skilled personnel and business development with strong commercial and strategic partnerships, keeping the business cost-efficient.

Shareholder Returns, Operational Excellence, and Global Relevance: Our conservative approach to assessing returns considers total lines extended, historical usage ratios, and a balanced book. Initial operations in Brazil take center stage, leveraging the country's pivotal role in commodities. Expansion plans include entering the USA market, followed by broader LATAM regions, and then globally.

Market Analysis

Industry Overview

The global commodity trading market is diverse, with significant demand for customized OTC trading solutions. Hedgers often face liquidity challenges in covering margin calls, while market makers seek platforms that support their pricing and risk management needs. Additionally, the global nature of commodities trading introduces FX risk, which needs to be effectively managed. The market for energy and metals is particularly significant due to their global demand and price volatility.

Product Offering

Platform Features

  • Real-Time Market Data: Accurate and timely data leveraging CQG’s feeds.
  • Unified Trading Interface: Seamlessly manage listed and OTC trades.
  • Advanced Analytics: Powerful charting and analytical tools for better decision-making.
  • Risk Management: Real-time calculation of Greeks, VaR, and other metrics to monitor and manage risk, including FX risk.
  • Order Management System (OMS): Integrated with CQG’s OMS for efficient order execution.
  • Margin Call Financing: Immediate capital to cover margin calls, preventing forced liquidation.
  • Market Maker Tools: Customizable volatility and pricing curves for market makers to manage market risk and books.
  • Lifecycle Management: Manage the complex lifecycle of exotic products, including daily events like barriers.
  • Portfolio Management: Integrated view of exchange-like trades and exotic products, with tools for trading out of the portfolio, running VaR, and stress scenarios.

Monetization Strategies

Platform Fees

  • Subscription Fees: Monthly or annual subscription fees for accessing the platform, with tiered levels based on access and features (e.g., basic, advanced, premium).
  • Module Fees: Separate fees for different modules within the platform, such as advanced analytics, risk management tools, market maker tools, and financing capabilities.

Commissions per Contract

  • Transaction Fees: Commission on each trade executed on the platform, either a fixed fee per contract or a percentage of the transaction value.
  • Volume-Based Discounts: Discounts on transaction fees based on trading volume to incentivize high-volume trading.

Spreads Between Customer Trade Price and Book

  • Bid-Ask Spread: Capture the spread between the bid and ask prices quoted to customers, adjusted based on market conditions and risk profile.
  • Market Making: Act as a market maker to profit from the difference between buy and sell prices, leveraging the platform's risk management capabilities.

Netting of the Book

  • Netting Efficiency: Reduce overall risk exposure and optimize capital usage by netting offsetting positions from multiple customers, sharing savings with customers.
  • Risk Management Fees: Charge for advanced risk management and netting services that reduce margin requirements and trading costs.

Trading of Volatility and Gamma Scalping

  • Volatility Trading: Utilize analytics and market data to identify and trade volatility opportunities, profiting from changes in implied volatility.
  • Gamma Scalping: Engage in gamma scalping by dynamically hedging options positions to capture small price movements in the underlying asset.

Interest on Initial Margin and Variation Margin

  • Margin Financing: Charge interest on capital provided for initial and variation margin requirements, especially during high volatility periods.
  • Flexible Financing Terms: Competitive interest rates and flexible repayment schedules to attract more customers and increase margin financing utilization.

Additional Value-Added Services

  • Consulting and Advisory: Provide consulting services to optimize trading strategies, manage risk, and comply with regulations, charging fees for these services.
  • Education and Training: Offer educational resources and training programs, including webinars, workshops, and certification programs, monetized through participation fees.

Data and Analytics

  • Data Subscriptions: Monetize market data and analytics by offering subscriptions or selling insights to third parties, such as research firms and financial analysts.

Organization Management & Legal Structure

Organizational Structure

The success of any business venture relies heavily on the expertise, leadership, and coordination of its management team. In this section, we provide an overview of Soma XT's initial organizational structure and the key individuals who drive the company's strategic vision and day-to-day operations.

As we navigate the complexities of the agricultural market, our organizational structure is designed to foster collaboration, efficiency, and accountability across all levels of the company. Each member of our management team brings unique skills and perspectives to their respective roles, contributing to Soma XT's mission of revolutionizing risk management and trading strategies in the agribusiness industry.

Key Roles and Responsibilities

  • Chief Executive Officer (CEO): Provides overall leadership and strategic direction for Soma XT. Drives the company's mission of revolutionizing risk management and trading strategies in the agricultural market. Responsible for setting clear goals, making high-level decisions, and ensuring the company's vision is effectively communicated and executed.
  • Chief Commercial Officer - Risk Management (CCO): Plays a critical role in driving revenue growth and expanding Soma XT's client base across multiple commodity sectors, including grains, energy, metals, and softs. Develops and executes strategic sales and marketing initiatives to capture market share and maximize profitability. Oversees customer relations, market research, branding initiatives, and the Trading Academy.
  • Chief Trading Officer (CTO): Central to driving the company's trading activities and optimizing its trading strategies across various commodity sectors. Responsible for developing and executing innovative trading models, managing the trade book, and maximizing returns while effectively managing risk.
  • Chief Operating Officer (COO): Instrumental in driving operational excellence, optimizing efficiency, and ensuring the seamless execution of the company's business activities. Oversees both traditional operational functions and strategic technology initiatives, aligning business operations with the company's strategic objectives and leveraging technology to enhance performance and innovation.
  • Chief Risk Officer (CRO): Plays a vital role in safeguarding the company's interests and ensuring the integrity of its operations amidst the complexities of the commodities market. Develops and implements robust risk management strategies to mitigate market risk and counterpart risk effectively.
  • Controller: Responsible for the financial reporting, accounting, and overall financial management of Soma XT. Ensures accurate financial records and compliance with regulatory requirements.
